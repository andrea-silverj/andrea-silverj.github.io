Welcome to my personal webpage!

I'm a computational evolutionary biologist
specialised in virome studies

I'm a PhD candidate at the University of Trento, currently working @Rota-Stabelli Lab (C3A department) and @Segata Lab (CIBIO department).

What did you say? Why am I interested in viruses?
Of course, because they are the most abundant and puzzling biological entities on our planet! Studying them is very fascinating.

Anyway, even though my main research interest is viral molecular evolution, I also collaborate with other scientists on projects involving different fields, such as phylogenomic studies about animals, plants and fungi.

Check the "Projects" section of this page to get an idea of what I do!

What do I do?



Studying viral evolution on a large scale

Status: ongoing
Development of metagenomic and phylogenomic methods to study different groups of viruses.



Phage-host coevolution in metagenomes

Status: ongoing
I'm studying how phages and bacteria interact in their natural environment.



Codon usage bias in viruses

Status: ongoing
Trying to understand how viruses use the genetic code.



SARS-CoV-2 evolution in Trentino

Status: completed
I characterised the spread and evolution of the coronavirus in the Trentino region (Italy) for the first two waves of the epidemic (2019/2021).



Molecular evolution of arboviruses

Status: completed
I performed various evolutionary studies on viruses transmitted by arthropods, in particular West Nile virus and Zika virus.



Tick virome

Status: ongoing
I'm exploring viromes of ticks from different Italian populations.



Side projects and collaborations



The samurai wasp genome project

Status: ongoing
I'm studying the genomics of the parasitoid species Trissolcus japonicus, one of the natural enemies of the brown marmorated stink bug, which is a dangerous invasive species.



Mosquito genomes and metagenomes

Status: completed
I contributed to a genome skimming and microbial screening of Aedes koreicus and Aedes japonicus.



Phylogenomics of Fagaceae (with CNR)

Status: completed
The history of 2 Italian populations of beeches was reconstructed from plastid data through molecular clocks.
